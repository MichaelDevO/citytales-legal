Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 13, 2026

City Tales helps users explore Polish cities through walking routes, map checkpoints, questions, facts, points, and progress tracking.

This policy explains what data the app uses, why it is used, and how users can request deletion of their data.

Data We Collect Or Process

City Tales may collect or process the following data when Firebase is enabled and the user signs in:

  • Account information: display name, email address, profile photo URL, and Firebase user identifier from Google Sign-In or Apple Sign-In.
  • Progress data: selected city, selected route, completed points, awarded points, route completion status, and related timestamps.
  • App activity: route interactions, completion events, and basic app usage events used to understand and improve the app.
  • App diagnostics: crash logs, performance diagnostics, device information, and technical events used to find and fix issues.
  • Device or app identifiers: Firebase installation identifiers, analytics identifiers, App Check signals, and authentication-related identifiers.
  • Location data: the app uses device location to show the user's position and decide whether the user is near a route point.

The current app does not collect user-uploaded photos or videos.

Location

City Tales asks for location permission because routes are based on real places on a map. Location is used to:

  • show the user's position on the map,
  • estimate distance to the next route point,
  • unlock questions when the user is close enough to a place.

The app should not store the user's precise GPS history in Firestore. Route progress is stored as completed route points, not as a continuous location track.

Why We Use Data

Data is used for:

  • app functionality, including sign-in, progress sync, route completion, and point awards,
  • account management,
  • fraud prevention, security, and abuse prevention through Firebase App Check and authentication,
  • analytics and diagnostics,
  • crash reporting and reliability improvements,
  • future product improvements.

Third-Party Services

City Tales uses third-party services and SDKs, including:

  • Firebase Authentication,
  • Cloud Firestore,
  • Firebase App Check,
  • Firebase Analytics,
  • Firebase Crashlytics,
  • Firebase Remote Config,
  • Google Sign-In,
  • Sign in with Apple,
  • MapLibre and OpenStreetMap-based map tiles.

These providers may process data according to their own privacy terms and as needed to provide app functionality, authentication, diagnostics, analytics, or map display.

Data Sharing

City Tales does not sell personal data.

Data may be processed by service providers that help operate the app, such as Firebase/Google services and Apple sign-in services. Data may also be disclosed if required by law, security requirements, or abuse prevention.

Data Retention

Account and progress data may be kept while the user account is active or while needed to provide app functionality. Diagnostic and analytics data may be kept according to Firebase/Google retention settings.

Data Deletion

Users can request deletion of their City Tales account data and progress data by contacting:

citytales.support@gmail.com

The request should include the email address used to sign in to City Tales.

Account deletion instructions are also available at City Tales Account Deletion.

Security

City Tales uses Firebase services, authentication, App Check, Firestore security rules, and encrypted network connections where supported to protect app data. No system can be guaranteed to be completely secure, but the app is designed to limit access to user progress data.

Children's Privacy

City Tales is intended as a general audience app for exploring cities. It is not designed specifically for children and should not knowingly collect data from children without appropriate consent where required by law.

Changes To This Policy

This policy may be updated as the app changes, for example when new cities, routes, paid features, partner routes, or account features are added.

The latest version is published at the public privacy policy URL used in Google Play Console and App Store Connect.
